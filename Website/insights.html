Some key findings ...

1. Urban-rural divide

A denser colour pattern can be noticed in several cities, particularly in Belgium, Switzerland and South-West Germany.

This was likely due to a higher number of streets present in cities than the countryside, forests and mountainous regions.

Using our map visualisation, try to spot cities such as Rennes or Bordeaux in France, Rome or Napoli in Italy, Linz or Vienna in Austria and Rostock or Dresden in Germany!



2. Nature-related toponyms

There is a high number of streets named after plants or animals species, most prominently observed in Germanophone countries.

Hover over the image below!

This may be partly due to a legacy of 1850s Romanticism (under which nature was valorised) and post-WWII renaming of streets under German reunification.
