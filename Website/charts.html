How do I use this page?

The chart on the left depicts the proportion of street names in each category across 7 countries. The proportion is calculated by dividing the number of streets in the specific thematic category by the total number of labelled streets in the country.

The specific statistics for each country may be seen by hovering over the respective bars.

Click on the countries to select/deselect them from showing on the graph.


The chart on the right shows the historical accumulation of street names across 7 countries. The x-axis shows the time period of analysis (1600 to 2020), and the y-axis value corresponds with all street names associated with an individual born before the specific time period. The percentage shown is calculated by dividing the number of such streets by the total number of streets in the country.

Click on the countries to select/deselect them from showing on the graph. Hover over a point to show its precise percentage.


To explore other pages, use the navigation buttons on the left side of the page.


I think the data is incorrect.


Our analysis relied on data from OpenStreetMap and WikiData API, and thus is only appropriate for a broad approximation of street toponymics in Western Europe. Besides the issues with incorrect or incomplete open data, language barriers may have impeded the accuracy of the analysis. Nevertheless, we endeavoured to give a broad, accurate representation of street names in Western Europe based on our available data.
